The following is my honest review of the 9th mainline Resident Evil Game : Requiem

---
### RE - Requiem
#### The Story

Worthy of any Resident evil movie, but one of the better ones.
Being told through the perspective of both protagonists helped a lot with suspense and cliffhangers.


#### The Gameplay

Alright, so the game has two protagonists, Leon and Grace.

Leon playing like his RE4 Remake late game version, and Grace playing life an Ethan in between Re7 and Re8.

The concept is interesting and well executed, since the formula is built around Grace sneaking through the spooky new resident evil formula, and switching to Leon for the cleanup once she's done with an area.

This helps with the game's pacing, but bring is a new problem that i've only encountered in one other game ...


#### Resident Evil 9 is unsatisfying

While it manages to feature every mechanic from the series, starting with the re7 sneaking-survival focus, with a tidy bit of action close to re8's, to the crimson heads from Re1 remake, the Re4 remake parry and combat packed sequences.
It even brings back Raccoon City Police Department, Lickers, Tyrans, Nemesis, Re5 vehicle chases, the merchant (replaced by AI), and i absolutely forgot some.

The game doesn't squeeze them to death like an older game would, it barely uses them as fan service for some. The difficulty of the game doesn't increase throughout as we unlock new weapons and tools, face scarier enemies, because Leon is the scariest enemy in the game.
The tension and survival parts are not the focus, they're one of the focuses, the progression that comes with it breaks up the moment we switch to Leon, and it only gets harder and harder to take it seriously again.

#### Resident Evil 9 is the last re game

Or at least it feels like it, every element is there, Wesker, Re6 wink wink, Las Plagas, Tyrans, one final and ultimate antivirus, Leon becoming Old Snake from MGS4, the game feels like you're playing the last game in a saga which is euphoric in a sense, it even caught my fanboy feelings by the end.



#### And while the game is very fun at the beginning, a bit lackluster in the middle, good again by the end, it feels to me like an incomplete experience, lacking the intensity from Re2 remake, or the progression of Re4 and its remake to stand along the best games of the series.

### Resident Evil Requiem thus acquire the ranking : Mid, and THIS is how i would make it better with minimum changes :

Keep the game's first part, the Asylum or whatever as it is, maybe force the player to go back to the first parts where they started to play around the ptsd of meeting that tracker again, and maybe have her come back a bit earlier to force the player to use their magnum ammo as an Alien Isolation stay away flamethrower, making crafting also feel heavier.

Next, the actual stinker, Leon should pass out when he reaches Raccoon City, either after fighting that spider and not killing it, or from the get go.
The idea is to play at dusk, less and less light, an increasingly difficult and stressful experience.

Next step, the spider starts chasing you, you need to find the weapon the BSAA used to take one of its kind down. Now that they're all wasted, the spiders are controlling their hideouts, and Leon needs to find a way in, gather information, find a way to get to the town center, while fighting a mix of those zombies and spiders AND dodging the big ones.
When you get the BSAA weapon or special ammo, damage the spider and leave it with a gaping wound, then when it appears again all healed up, the player will get confused and terrorized to see another (injured one from earlier) join the boss fight.

Leon should have hallucinations, once he reaches RCPD (no bike chase scene thank you, either pass out and get picked up by BSAA (who then get killed by a Tyran (what a cool idea to tease it)), Leon goes from old RCPD to new, advancing in a mix of psychosis and delirium, sometimes waste ammo on imagined zombie cops, or zombie dogs from his past, or get bit by real ones.

This would make the player paranoid, and since all the assets are in Re2 remake, technically this would take little time and money to cook.

Next step, the tyran tracks us, sometimes it's mister X, sometimes an actual tyran, leading us to the BSAA hideout, a final boss fight with the nightmare to cleanly close this one.

Then, Grace should get to sneak around for a bit longer, a lot of Leon's exploration is boring, have Grace play out a bit more, have the Lickers randomly jump forward to teach the player never to stand in front of them. Make the zombies afraid of the light still, to play further with this, it was a great idea.

Finally, once Leon shows up all sick and tired, give him a stamina bar, make parries and running precious, take away the gameplay for a bit, and force the players to play The Evil Within for a tiny amount.

### Final Thoughts :
I might have been a bit harsh, the game is good, though i expected better and was worried they'd go the re6 direction again.
Next, make an Evi Within game or a Dead Space game Capcom ! Thank you.
